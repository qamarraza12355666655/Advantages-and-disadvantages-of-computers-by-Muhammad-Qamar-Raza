    <!-- Advantages Section -->
    <div class="section advantages">
        <h2>Advantages of Computers</h2>
        
        <h3>1. High Speed and Efficiency</h3>
        <p>Computers can process millions of instructions per second. Tasks that would take humans days or weeks to complete—such as complex mathematical calculations or data analysis—are executed in a fraction of a second.</p>

        <h3>2. Massive Data Storage</h3>
        <p>A computer can store vast amounts of information (documents, images, videos, and databases) in a very small physical space. This eliminates the need for massive paper filing systems and makes retrieving data instant.</p>

        <h3>3. Automation and Productivity</h3>
        <p>Computers can handle repetitive and mundane tasks without getting tired. In industries like manufacturing, banking, and software development, automation increases production rates and reduces human error.</p>

        <h3>4. Global Connectivity and Communication</h3>
        <p>Through the internet, computers connect people across the globe. Emails, video conferencing, and instant messaging allow for real-time collaboration, making remote work and long-distance relationships effortless.</p>

        <h3>5. Learning and Entertainment</h3>
        <p>Computers are incredible tools for education, offering access to online courses, research papers, and interactive learning. They also serve as a hub for entertainment, including gaming, streaming movies, and digital art.</p>
    </div>

    <!-- Disadvantages Section -->
    <div class="section disadvantages">
        <h2>Disadvantages of Computers</h2>
        
        <h3>1. Health Issues</h3>
        <p>Prolonged computer use can lead to several physical health problems due to poor posture and repetitive motions.</p>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Eye Strain:</strong> Looking at a screen for hours can cause blurred vision and headaches.</li>
            <li><strong>Carpal Tunnel Syndrome:</strong> Repetitive typing and mouse clicking can strain the wrists.</li>
            <li><strong>Sedentary Lifestyle:</strong> Sitting for long periods contributes to obesity, back pain, and cardiovascular issues.</li>
        </ul>

        <h3>2. Cyber Security and Privacy Risks</h3>
        <p>Being connected to the internet exposes users to malicious threats. Hackers can steal personal data, credit card information, and identities through malware, phishing, and ransomware.</p>

        <h3>3. Impact on Employment (Job Displacement)</h3>
        <p>As computers and Artificial Intelligence (AI) become more advanced, they automate tasks previously done by humans. This can lead to job displacement in sectors like manufacturing, data entry, and customer service.</p>

        <h3>4. Distraction and Addiction</h3>
        <p>Computers offer endless entertainment, which can become a major distraction. Social media, gaming, and endless browsing can lead to decreased productivity, shorter attention spans, and computer addiction (especially among younger users).</p>

        <h3>5. E-Waste and Environmental Impact</h3>
        <p>Computers quickly become obsolete, leading to massive amounts of electronic waste (e-waste). If not recycled properly, toxic chemicals from computer hardware can leach into the soil and water, harming the environment.</p>
